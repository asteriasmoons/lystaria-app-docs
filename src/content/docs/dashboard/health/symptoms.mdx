## Overview Card
At the top of the page, the Overview card gives you a quick snapshot of your symptom activity.

<img src="/img/screenshots/health/symptoms/view.png" class="docs-image-single"/>

It shows:
- Total number of logged symptoms
- Number of symptoms logged this week

This allows you to quickly understand how often you are experiencing symptoms without needing to scroll through your full history.

<DotDivider />

## Symptom Entries
Below the overview, your symptom entries are displayed as individual cards.

Each entry shows:
- The date and time the symptom was logged
- The severity level
- The selected symptom tags
- An optional note if one was added

This layout keeps everything easy to scan while still giving you meaningful context for each log.

<DotDivider />

## Logging a Symptom
To log a new symptom, tap the plus button in the top right of the page header.

<div class="docs-image-row">
<img src="/img/screenshots/health/symptoms/add1.png" class="docs-image-single"/>
<img src="/img/screenshots/health/symptoms/add2.png" class="docs-image-single"/>
</div>

This opens the Log Symptoms popup where you can quickly create a new entry.

Inside this popup, you will:
- Select a date and time
- Choose one or more symptoms
- Set a severity level
- Optionally add a note

Once you tap save, the entry is added to your list immediately.

<DotDivider />

## Symptom Selection
The Symptom Logger includes a wide range of selectable symptoms to choose from.

These are displayed as selectable tags, allowing you to quickly tap and select everything that applies. You can choose multiple symptoms for a single entry, making it easy to capture more complex experiences.

This approach keeps logging fast while still giving you flexibility in how you describe what you are feeling.

<DotDivider />

## Severity Levels
Each entry includes a severity level to help you describe how intense the symptom feels.

The available levels are:
- 1 -- Mild
- 2 -- Noticeable
- 3 -- Moderate
- 4 -- Significant
- 5 -- Severe

This creates a consistent scale that helps you compare symptoms over time. Instead of guessing how bad something felt, you build a repeatable reference point for your experiences.

<DotDivider />

## Notes
Each symptom entry includes an optional notes field.

This allows you to add context such as:
- What you were doing before the symptom started
- Possible triggers like stress, food, or environment
- How long the symptom lasted
- Anything else that feels important

These notes turn simple logs into meaningful insight, especially when reviewing patterns later.

<DotDivider />

## Entry Details
Tapping on a symptom entry opens a detailed view.

<img src="/img/screenshots/health/symptoms/details.png" class="docs-image-single"/>

In this view, you can see:
- The full date and time
- The exact severity level
- All selected symptoms
- Any notes that were added

This gives you a clean, focused breakdown of a single moment without distraction.

<DotDivider />

## Managing Entries
Each symptom entry can be deleted if needed.

From the detail view, you have the option to remove an entry. This allows you to correct mistakes or clean up your history when necessary.

Because this is a personal tracking system, you always remain in control of your data.

<DotDivider />

## Why This Matters
Tracking symptoms consistently can reveal patterns that are easy to miss in day-to-day life.

Over time, you may begin to notice:
- Certain symptoms happening at specific times
- Triggers that cause flare-ups
- Changes in severity across days or weeks

This kind of awareness can help you make more informed decisions about your health, routines, and habits.

It can also be incredibly useful when communicating with healthcare providers, giving you a clear and organized way to explain what you have been experiencing.

<GradientDivider />

The Symptom Logger is a simple but powerful tool for understanding your body over time. By logging symptoms, tracking severity, and adding context when needed, you create a record that supports awareness, clarity, and better decision-making in your daily life.

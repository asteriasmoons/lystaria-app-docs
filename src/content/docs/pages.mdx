## Single pages
Single pages are exactly what they sound like. They are pages that stand on their own and do not contain other pages inside of them.

These pages are directly accessible and are typically found in the bottom navigation bar. When you open a single page, what you see is everything that page has to offer. There are no additional layers to explore within it.

Single pages are simple, direct, and easy to access. They act as the foundation of the app’s structure.

<DotDivider />

## Nested pages
Nested pages are pages that live inside other pages. These are accessed through icon buttons in the header of an anchor page.

When you enter a page and see a circular icon button in the header, that is your indicator that there is another page available. Tapping that icon takes you deeper into the app.

Nested pages can also contain other nested pages. This means the structure can go multiple layers deep. If you are exploring and see another icon button in the header, there is likely another page waiting there as well.

<DotDivider />

## Anchor pages
Anchor pages are the pages that connect everything together. These are the main pages you access from the bottom navigation bar, and they act as entry points into nested pages.

When you are on an anchor page, you will still see the bottom navigation bar. This is one of the easiest ways to tell where you are in the app.

Anchor pages are not just pages. They are gateways to deeper functionality.

<DotDivider />

## How to tell where you are
There are a few simple ways to tell whether you are on an anchor page or a nested page.

If you see the bottom navigation bar, you are on an anchor page.

If the bottom navigation bar is gone, you are inside a nested page.

Nested pages also include a left-pointing arrow bubble button at the top left of the screen. This button allows you to go back to the previous page. It acts as your way out of the current layer and back into the main structure.

<DotDivider />

## Navigating nested pages
To exit a nested page, use the left-pointing arrow bubble button in the top left corner. This will take you back to the anchor page you came from.

Because nested pages can exist within other nested pages, you may need to step back through multiple layers depending on how deep you have gone. Each layer brings you back one step at a time.

<DotDivider />

## Guided exploration
If you do not want to search for nested pages on your own, you can use the built-in feature tour.

Go to the Profile page and turn on the option to rerun the feature tour. Once enabled, when you visit an anchor page that contains nested pages, the icon button in the header will be highlighted.

A small box will appear explaining what that page is for. You will see options for dismiss and next. Choosing next will continue guiding you through the app, while dismiss will stop the tour for that page.

This is a simple way to discover features without needing to remember where everything is.

<DotDivider />

## Understanding the system
Once you become familiar with single pages, anchor pages, and nested pages, the app begins to make more sense as a whole.

You may find that you do not use every feature, and that is completely fine. The goal is not to use everything, but to have everything available when you need it.

<GradientDivider />

Lystaria was built so you do not need to rely on multiple apps to manage different parts of your life. Everything is brought together into one system that you can explore at your own pace.

It will not be for everyone, and that is okay. But for the people who have been looking for something that brings everything into one place, this structure is what makes that possible.

## Calendar View
The calendar view holds all of your event cards for events you have created. These events are displayed within your selected calendar view, giving you a clear layout of what is scheduled.

In the top right corner of the page, you will find the calendar settings menu. This menu allows you to add a new calendar, filter which calendars are visible, manage your existing calendars, join a shared event, and turn on or manage Apple Calendar sync. Keeping all of these options in one place makes it easier to control how your calendar system works.

<DotDivider />

## Adding Events
Each day in the week view includes a plus sign button that allows you to quickly create a new event. This makes adding events feel natural as you move through your schedule.

When creating an event, you can choose which calendar it belongs to, add a title, set your dates, and optionally turn on an event reminder. You can also configure repeating behavior with flexible scheduling options, allowing events to repeat at different intervals based on your needs.

Additional options include searching for a location or using your current location, adding a meeting URL, writing a description, and marking the event as shared. If you enable sharing, the event becomes part of a shared experience rather than something only you can see.

## Week and Day Views
The Calendar includes both a week view and a day view to give you different perspectives on your schedule.

<div class="docs-image-row">
<img src="/img/screenshots/info/weekview1.png" class="docs-image-single"/>
<img src="/img/screenshots/info/weekview2.png" class="docs-image-single"/>
</div>

When entering the calendar, the view will automatically scroll to the current day or time. This removes the need to search for where you are in your schedule and keeps the experience focused on the present moment.

<div class="docs-image-row">
<img src="/img/screenshots/info/dayview1.png" class="docs-image-single"/>
<img src="/img/screenshots/info/dayview2.png" class="docs-image-single"/>
</div>

To switch to the day view, tap the calendar month title in the header. To return to the week view, tap the date title in the header. Events can be created and managed from both views, so you are not limited to one way of interacting with your schedule.

<DotDivider />

## Event Details and Overview
Tapping on an event card opens a detailed popup that displays all of the information you entered. This includes the title, date, time, location, description, and any other options you selected when creating the event.

<div class="docs-image-row">
<img src="/img/screenshots/info/details.png" class="docs-image-single"/>
<img src="/img/screenshots/info/overview.png" class="docs-image-single"/>
</div>

At the bottom of this popup, there is an Overview button. This takes you to a summary view of your event activity, showing counts such as completed today, this week, this month, this year, and custom totals. This gives you a broader perspective on how your events are being completed over time.

<GradientDivider />

The Calendar in Lystaria is designed to be flexible, connected, and easy to navigate. With multiple views, detailed event creation, shared event support, and built-in summaries, it gives you a complete system for managing your time and experiences in one place.

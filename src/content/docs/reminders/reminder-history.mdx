## What the History View is For
The History View exists to give you visibility into your past reminder activity. It is not a separate system, but an extension of your reminders that allows you to look backward instead of only focusing on what is due right now.

Instead of losing track of what happened during your day, you can open this view and immediately see how things went. This makes it easier to stay aware of your habits and understand your behavior over time.

It also helps remove uncertainty. You don’t have to question whether you completed something or try to remember how your day played out. The information is already there, laid out in a way that is easy to scan and understand.

## Using the History Calendar
The History Calendar is how you move through your timeline.

You can tap on any day to see that day’s activity, and you can move backward through time to revisit previous days. Your history begins the moment you start logging reminders, and from that point forward it continues to grow automatically.

This means that in the future, you will be able to go all the way back to when you first started using the system. Over time, this becomes more valuable, because you are no longer just seeing individual days -- you are seeing patterns.

The calendar gives structure to that experience. It turns your reminder history into something you can navigate instead of something that just exists in the background.

<DotDivider />

## What Gets Tracked
Each day shows a clear breakdown of your reminder activity.

- Completed reminders  
- Skipped reminders  

This allows you to see the full picture of your day, not just the parts that went well. Both sides matter, because they tell you something real about your energy, your priorities, and your follow-through.

Instead of only focusing on completion, this view shows you your actual behavior. That makes it more honest, and because of that, more useful.

<DotDivider />

## Connected Systems
The History View does not only track standard reminders.

- Habit reminders  
- Event reminders  

If you didn’t user regular reminders, but you completed habit reminders or had event-based reminders, those will still appear here. This ensures that your history remains complete and connected across the entire reminders system.

Everything flows into one place. This prevents gaps in your timeline and allows you to understand your activity without needing to jump between different features.

<DotDivider />

## Why the History View Matters
The History View helps you understand your patterns over time. When you can see what you’ve been doing day after day, it becomes easier to recognize consistency, notice changes, and adjust where needed.

It also helps you stay grounded. Instead of relying on how you feel about your progress, you can see it clearly. That removes guesswork and replaces it with something tangible.

Over time, this turns into awareness. And that awareness is what allows real change to happen.

<GradientDivider />

The History View gives your reminders system memory. It takes your daily actions and preserves them in a way that you can return to at any time. That alone makes it one of the most valuable supporting views in the app.

It works because it stays simple while still being meaningful. You are not overwhelmed with unnecessary detail, but you are given enough information to understand your behavior clearly.

If you want to see how your routines are actually playing out over time, this is the view that shows you.

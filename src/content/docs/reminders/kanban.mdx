## Understanding the Kanban structure
At its simplest, the Kanban view is made up of boards, columns, and cards. Each card represents a reminder, and each column represents a category that you define. This gives you a system where your reminders are grouped visually instead of being stacked in a single list.

You are not required to follow traditional Kanban structures. You do not have to use columns like "To-Do," "In Progress," or "Done." The system allows you to define columns in a way that actually makes sense for your own routines and responsibilities.

<GradientDivider />

## A visual way to interact with reminders
The Kanban view exists to give you another way to interact with your reminders. Just like the Time Blocking view, you can complete reminders directly within this view. This means you are not limited to one place when it comes to managing your tasks.

## Custom boards that reflect your life
You can create boards for whatever you need. This is where the Kanban view becomes especially personal. Instead of being locked into a predefined structure, you can design boards that reflect how your responsibilities actually exist.

<img src="/img/screenshots/kanban/column.png" class="docs-image-small"/>

For example, a board might be structured around different types of responsibilities rather than progress stages. Columns could represent routines, habits, events, custom tasks, or appointments. This kind of structure can feel far more natural than forcing everything into a generic workflow.

<GradientDivider />

## Moving beyond traditional "Done" systems
Traditional Kanban systems often rely on a "Done" column, but that idea does not always fit well with how reminders behave in Lystaria. Many reminders are recurring, which means they are not meant to be completed once and then forgotten.

Because of this, it often makes more sense to organize reminders by type or purpose rather than by completion stage. A recurring reminder does not belong in a permanent "Done" state, because it will need to be completed again the next time it appears. The Kanban view supports this by allowing you to define structures that better match how recurring systems actually work.

<GradientDivider />

## Designed to feel engaging
Design plays a major role in the Kanban experience. Seeing your reminders as cards inside a structured visual space creates a different kind of interaction. It can feel more engaging and more satisfying than working through a traditional list.

This is not just about appearance. The visual layout helps reduce overwhelm by grouping related reminders together and making everything easier to scan. It creates a sense of order without forcing you into a rigid system.

<GradientDivider />

## Multiple ways to navigate your reminders
The Kanban view is part of a larger system where reminders can be accessed and interacted with in multiple ways. You can complete reminders from the main list, from the Time Blocking view, or from the Kanban view itself.

Each view offers a different perspective. The Kanban view focuses on visual grouping and organization, giving you a way to see your reminders in a more structured and categorized format.

<GradientDivider />

## A system that adapts to you
The Kanban view is not about following a strict methodology. It is about giving you a structure that you can shape into something that fits your life. Whether you organize by type, category, or something more personal, the system allows you to define that for yourself.

This flexibility is what makes the Kanban view powerful. It does not assume how you should work. Instead, it gives you the tools to build a layout that feels natural, clear, and satisfying to use.

<GradientDivider />

The Kanban view transforms reminders into a visual system of boards and cards, giving you a flexible and engaging way to interact with your responsibilities. With customizable boards, adaptable column structures, support for recurring reminders, and the ability to complete tasks directly within the view, it provides a powerful alternative to traditional list-based organization.

If you prefer a more visual and structured approach to managing your reminders, the Kanban view offers a space where organization and flexibility come together in a way that feels intuitive and rewarding.

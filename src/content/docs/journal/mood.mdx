## Logging Your Mood
Logging a mood is designed to feel natural rather than rigid. You are not forced into a strict format, but guided into capturing what actually reflects your experience.

You can select one or more moods from a wide range of emotional states, choose activities that give context to your day, and optionally write a note. These pieces come together to form a more complete picture of how you felt in that moment.

## Moods and Activities
Moods are not limited to a single choice. You can select multiple emotions at once, which allows you to capture more nuanced states like feeling both tired and content, or overwhelmed but still motivated.

Activities add context. They help you understand what was happening around you when you felt a certain way. Over time, this becomes one of the most powerful parts of the system, because it allows patterns to form between what you do and how you feel.

<DotDivider />

## Notes and Expression
The note field is completely open. You can write as much or as little as you want, or skip it entirely. It is there for moments when your thoughts go beyond simple mood labels.

Sometimes the moods are enough. Other times, you need space to explain what is underneath them. This flexibility allows journaling and mood tracking to blend together when needed.

<DotDivider />

## One Log Per Day
The Mood Logger is designed around a daily rhythm. You can log your mood once per day, which encourages consistency without turning it into something overwhelming or repetitive.

This also gives each day a single emotional snapshot. Instead of scattering entries, you build a clean timeline of how your days felt over time.

<DotDivider />

## The Three Core Metrics
Every mood log generates three underlying metrics that help you understand your emotional state in a more structured way.

<img src="/img/screenshots/mood/popup.png" class="doc-image-single"/>

#### Mood Score
Mood Score reflects the overall emotional quality of your day. It is calculated based on the moods you select and gives you a simple number that represents how your day felt as a whole.

Higher scores represent more positive emotional states, while lower scores reflect heavier or more difficult days. It is not meant to judge your emotions, but to help you notice patterns over time.

#### Emotional Tone
Emotional Tone captures the direction of your emotions. It shows whether your overall state leaned positive or negative, and how strongly it leaned in that direction.

Even neutral feelings still carry a slight direction, which is why this value always leans one way or the other. This makes it easier to see whether your emotional baseline is shifting over time.

#### Intensity
Intensity measures how much emotional energy was present, regardless of whether the emotions were positive or negative.

A calm, quiet day and an energized, expressive day might both feel good, but they are very different experiences. Tracking intensity separately allows you to understand not just how you felt, but how strongly you felt it.

<GradientDivider />

## Mood Insights
Once you have logged a few entries, the Mood Insights card appears at the top of the page. This gives you a broader view of your emotional patterns.

It shows things like your most common moods, the activities you engage in most often, and averages for your score, tone, and intensity. Instead of looking at individual days, this helps you understand your overall emotional trends.

You can also tap into the insights card to learn more about what each metric means and how to interpret it.

<DotDivider />

## Mood Streaks
Below the insights card is the Mood Streak tracker. This shows how consistently you have been logging your mood.

Your current streak reflects how many days in a row you have logged, while your best streak shows the longest run you have ever achieved. The system also shows whether your streak is active or waiting, depending on whether you have logged for the current day.

This creates a gentle sense of momentum without adding pressure.

<DotDivider />

## Today and History
The Mood Logger separates your current state from your past entries.

The Today view shows your most recent mood log in a structured format, including your metrics, selected moods, activities, and note. It is a focused snapshot of how you are feeling right now.

The History view allows you to look back at previous logs. Entries are shown in small groups and can be expanded over time, making it easy to scroll through your emotional history without feeling overwhelmed.

## Reading and Reflecting
Each mood entry is designed to be readable at a glance but also expandable for deeper reflection. You can revisit past days, compare how you felt, and start to recognize patterns in both your emotions and your behavior.

Over time, this turns your mood logs into something more meaningful than individual entries. It becomes a record of your emotional landscape.

<DotDivider />

## Deleting a Log
If needed, you can delete a mood log from your history. This removes it from your visible data while keeping the system stable behind the scenes.

Deleting a log updates your current state as well. If you remove the only log for today, the app reflects that no mood has been logged yet.

<DotDivider />

## Widget and System Integration
The Mood Logger connects with other parts of Lystaria. When you log your mood, the system updates your widgets so you can see whether you have logged for the day without opening the app.

It also contributes to your overall progress within the app, rewarding consistency and encouraging you to keep showing up for yourself.

<DotDivider />

## Self-care Points
Each mood log contributes to your Self-Care Points. These points accumulate over time and reflect your consistency in tracking and understanding your emotional state.

This ties the Mood Logger into the larger system of growth within Lystaria, turning small daily actions into long-term progress.

The Mood Logger in Lystaria is designed to help you understand yourself over time. By combining emotional labels, context, reflection, and structured insights, it transforms simple check-ins into something much deeper. It is not just about recording how you feel, but about learning from it.

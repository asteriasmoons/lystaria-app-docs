## What Habits Are
A habit is something you want to do consistently over time. It is not tied to a single moment like a reminder, but instead represents a repeated behavior that builds up over days, weeks, and longer periods.

Research around habit formation often emphasizes repetition and consistency as the foundation of long-term change. Small actions done regularly tend to be more sustainable than large actions done occasionally. The Habits system is built around that idea, helping you focus on showing up rather than being perfect.

<DotDivider />

## Habits Vs Reminders
Habits and reminders are connected, but they are not the same thing. A reminder is usually tied to a specific time and exists to prompt you to take action. A habit, on the other hand, is about the action itself and how often you repeat it.

You can attach reminders to habits, and when you do, they will appear in your reminder views just like any other reminder. This allows habits to integrate into your schedule when needed, while still maintaining their identity as something ongoing rather than one-time or strictly time-bound.

<DotDivider />

## Creating Habits
You can create new habits using the large floating plus button. The creation flow is designed to be flexible so it can support simple habits as well as more structured ones.

<img src="/img/screenshots/habits/newh3.png" class="docs-image-single"/>

Habits can be scheduled in multiple ways. You can set them to repeat daily, weekly, every set number of hours, or every set number of minutes. This allows you to define habits that match your actual routines rather than forcing everything into a single pattern.

The title and description you choose for your habit are also what will appear in notifications. This keeps everything consistent and ensures that what you see in the app is the same as what you see when you are reminded.

<DotDivider />

## Progress and Completion
Each habit includes a progress bar and dot indicators that reflect how much of the habit has been completed for the day. This gives you a visual sense of progress without needing to think about it manually.

For more complex habits, such as hydration, progress builds gradually. Each time you complete part of the habit, the progress bar fills a little more. This makes it easier to track habits that are not completed in a single action but instead build up over time.

Habits can also be completed from any reminder view if reminders are enabled for that habit. This allows you to interact with your habits from multiple places without breaking your flow.

<DotDivider />

## Skipping and Flexibility
Not every day will go as planned, and the Habits system accounts for that. You can skip a habit by tapping the skip button, and you can undo that skip just as easily by tapping it again.

This flexibility is important because it allows you to stay honest with your habits without feeling locked into a rigid system. Skipping does not break the structure. It simply reflects reality.

<DotDivider />

## Clearing and Resetting
Each habit card includes a clear button and a reset button, and they serve different purposes.

The clear button removes the progress you have made for the current day. This resets the progress bar and indicator dots without affecting your longer-term tracking.

The reset button, on the other hand, resets your streak count back to zero. This is useful if you want to start fresh or redefine how you approach that habit going forward.

<DotDivider />

## Streak Tracking
The streaks card tracks how consistently you are completing your habits over time. It shows how many days and weeks you have maintained a habit, giving you a sense of momentum.

Streaks are not just about numbers. They reflect consistency and commitment. Seeing a streak grow can be motivating, but it also gives you a clear picture of how often you are actually following through.

<DotDivider />

## History and Reflection
Each habit includes a history view that you can access from the habit card. This allows you to look back at your past completions and see how your consistency has changed over time.

You can also manage your history directly. By long pressing a history entry, you can delete it if needed. This gives you control over your data and allows you to adjust things if something was logged incorrectly.

The Habits system in Lystaria is built to support consistency without rigidity. With flexible scheduling, progress tracking, streaks, history, and integration with reminders, it gives you the tools to build routines that actually fit your life. Over time, these small repeated actions become something more meaningful, shaping your days in a way that feels intentional and sustainable.

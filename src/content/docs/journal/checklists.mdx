## Creating checklist items
Each checklist item is created individually using the floating gradient plus button. This keeps the process quick and focused, allowing you to add items as they come to mind without extra steps.

<img src="/img/screenshots/checklists/view.png" class="docs-image-single"/>
<img src="/img/screenshots/checklists/newitem.png" class="docs-image-single"/>
</div>

When you are finished typing, simply press enter to return to the checklist view. The flow is designed to feel immediate and lightweight so that adding tasks never becomes a barrier.

<DotDivider />

## Completing items
Checklist items can be marked as completed with a simple tap. Once completed, they move naturally into your done state depending on the view you are using.

There are no notifications or reminders tied to checklist items. This keeps the system intentionally lightweight, allowing it to exist separately from the more structured Reminder and Habit systems.

<DotDivider />

## Checklist tabs
Checklists are organized into tabs rather than grouped inside cards. Each tab represents its own list, allowing you to separate different areas of your life or different types of tasks.

You can create a new tab using the white plus icon in the header. Once created, you can tap directly into the tab to rename it however you like.

<div class="docs-image-row">
<img src="/img/screenshots/checklists/newtab.png" class="docs-image-single"/>
<img src="/img/screenshots/checklists/deletetab.png" class="docs-image-single"/>

If you need to remove a tab, you can long press it to open a context menu with options to rename or delete. Deleting a tab will also delete all checklist items inside of it, so this action should be used carefully.

<DotDivider />

## Managing items
Each checklist item includes a menu accessed through the three dots button. From there, you can move the item to another tab or delete it entirely.

<img src="/img/screenshots/checklists/itemmenu.png" class="docs-image-single"/>

This keeps item management simple and consistent. Instead of needing multiple controls, everything related to that item lives in one place.

<DotDivider />

## Viewing your lists
Only four checklist items are displayed at a time. This keeps the interface clean and prevents long lists from becoming overwhelming.

If you have more items, you can reveal them by pressing the load more button at the bottom of the list. This allows you to expand your view when needed without cluttering the screen by default.

<DotDivider />

## List views
Each checklist tab includes three built-in views:

- **Active** -- shows items that are not yet completed  
- **Done** -- shows items that have been completed  
- **All** -- shows both active and completed items together  

You can switch between these views by tapping them. These views are fixed and cannot be modified, which keeps the system consistent and predictable across all tabs.

<GradientDivider />

The Checklists feature in Lystaria is designed to stay simple, flexible, and focused. By treating each item as its own entry and organizing lists through tabs and views, it creates a system that feels lightweight while still being structured enough to stay useful over time.

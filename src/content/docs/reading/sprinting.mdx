## Global Sprint Chat
Reading sprints take place inside a global chat. Anyone can participate, and anyone can start a sprint when one is not already running.

<img src="/img/screenshots/sprints/view.png" class="docs-image-single"/>

Only one sprint can run at a time. This keeps the experience shared and focused, so everyone who joins is participating in the same sprint session together.

<DotDivider />

## Starting Sprints
You can start a sprint using the bolt icon button in the bottom left of the sprint chat screen. From there, you choose the sprint duration and enter your starting page.

<img src="/img/screenshots/sprints/start.png" class="docs-image-single"/>

Once the sprint is created, there is a three-minute wait period before it officially begins. This gives other readers time to join, prepare, and enter their own starting page before the timer starts.

<DotDivider />

## Ending Sprints
When a sprint is running, a floating end sprint button appears in the bottom right of the screen. You can enter your ending page at any time during the sprint.

Your pages read are calculated from the starting and ending page numbers you entered. This allows the app to tally your progress for that sprint session and include it in the final sprint results.

<DotDivider />

## Sprint Results
After the sprint ends, the app creates a sprint card showing everyone who participated. Each reader’s pages read are tallied and displayed on the card.

The person who read the most pages appears at the top. This gives every sprint a clear result while still keeping the focus on shared reading and participation.

<DotDivider />

## Username
You can change your sprint username using the user icon in the chat page header. This is the name other readers will see when you participate in the chat, join sprints, and appear on sprint result cards.

This gives you control over how you show up in the global reading space while keeping your sprint identity easy to recognize.

<DotDivider />

## Chat Moderation
The sprint chat is global, so messages are shared with everyone using the feature. Only the app owner can clear chat messages.

This keeps chat cleanup controlled and prevents users from removing shared conversation history unexpectedly.

<DotDivider />

## Leaderboard
You can check the sprint leaderboard at any time using the trophy icon in the chat page header. The leaderboard shows how many points you have earned over time through sprint participation.

<img src="/img/screenshots/sprints/leaderboard.png" class="docs-image-single"/>

This gives the sprinting feature a long-term progress layer. Each sprint becomes part of a larger reading record, allowing you to see how your effort builds across sessions.

<GradientDivider />

Reading Sprints are built to make reading feel active, social, and rewarding. With global chat, timed sessions, sprint cards, usernames, and leaderboard points, the feature gives you a way to stay focused while sharing the experience with other readers.
